**ABI = Shared Data Infrastructure Layer**
- We build and maintain the upstream data model (contract, spend, admin fee, supplier-parent, and programmatic crosswalks)

Instead of proposing a Fusion Team model in email, **show** the overlap:

## Part 6: The Bigger Picture — ABI's Role Under Bill

What Matt described as ABI's role is precisely the right positioning:

> *"Upstream data modeling, advanced data enrichment, and maintaining the platform upon which we enable business technologists both within Brian's group, Justin's group, and others to help build and refine the final stages of the analytics that the sourcing team is going to depend upon."*

This maps perfectly to the org structure:

| ABI Provides | Business Partners Consume |
|-------------|--------------------------|
| Upstream data models (contract, spend, admin fee, supplier, program) | Brian's sourcing visualizations |
| Cross-reference dimensions (service line, supplier parent, commitment type) | Joe's Admin Fee baseline dashboards |
| Historical trending infrastructure (5+ years, month/quarter/FY) | Justin/Caitlin's Growth strategic readouts for Bruce |
| Advanced enrichment (alternative revenue, market share, penetration) | Each category team's drill-down analytics |
| Scalable platform (refresh cadence, governance, data quality) | All downstream consumers |

The key principle: **ABI is the foundation layer. Business owners decide what house gets built on it.** This avoids governance conflicts (ABI doesn't tell Justin how to present), eliminates duplication (one model serves many views), and makes ABI permanently essential.
