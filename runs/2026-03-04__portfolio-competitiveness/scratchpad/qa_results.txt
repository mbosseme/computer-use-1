## 1) Mathematical anomalies / impossible savings math

### A. Savings exceed “Annualized_Total_Spend” (impossible)
If **Annualized_Total_Spend** is our actual spend, then we **cannot** save more than that amount in a category. Yet several of your largest “opportunities” imply exactly that:

- **Interventional Sheaths and Introducers**
  - Total Spend: **$429.36M**
  - Savings Opp: **$346.43M**
  - Implied savings vs actual spend = **80.7%** (already extreme), BUT worse: your benchmarked spend is **$725.70M** which is **>$429.36M**, creating nonsense context for “gap to benchmark.”

- **Gastrointestinal Endoscopy Products**
  - Total Spend: **$88.60M**
  - Savings Opp: **$91.17M**  → **Savings > Total Spend (103%)** = **mathematical impossibility** if Total Spend is real.

These two alone make the portfolio targets questionable. Either:
1) **Annualized_Total_Spend is not actual spend**, or  
2) **Annualized_Benchmarked_Spend is not a subset of total**, or  
3) There is **duplication / incorrect annualization / incorrect unit mapping**, or  
4) “Savings” is not actually savings (e.g., it’s “gap to a target baseline” measured on a different population than Total Spend).

You need to reconcile definitions immediately, because the current presentation will produce unfair/invalid targets.

### B. Opportunity % denominator appears inconsistent with “Total Spend”
Your **Opportunity_Pct** is clearly calculated as:

> Opportunity_Pct = Savings / Annualized_Benchmarked_Spend

Example: Interventional Sheaths: 346.4 / 725.7 = 47.7% (matches)

That might be fine *if* “Annualized_Benchmarked_Spend” equals current eligible spend. But it does **not** behave like that, since it can exceed Total Spend. As shown, the percent can be “right” while the dollars are impossible relative to actual.

### C. Service line rollups likely inherit the same flaw
Service line Opportunity_Pct also uses the benchmarked spend denominator (e.g., PPI 603.5M / 2.528B = 23.9%). That’s mathematically consistent internally, but if benchmarked spend is overstated (or not comparable to actual spend), then **every service-line target is inflated/invalid**.

### B. Extremely high “opportunity %” values need validation against product mix
Opportunity % near/above 50% (GI Endoscopy 54%, Sheaths 47.7%, Surgical Instruments 48.9%) is not impossible in theory, but in practice it usually signals:

These are the exact categories I would **not** accept as targets until you show match logic and price normalization.

- What is **Annualized_Total_Spend** (actual paid, pre-rebate, post-rebate, includes freight, includes bill-only implants, etc.)?
