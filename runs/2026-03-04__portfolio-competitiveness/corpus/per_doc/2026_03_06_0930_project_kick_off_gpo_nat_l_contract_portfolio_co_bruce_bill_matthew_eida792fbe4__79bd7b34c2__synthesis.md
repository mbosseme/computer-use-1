This was a kickoff discussion for a high-priority initiative to strengthen the **national contract portfolio** before a large portion of it reaches expiration. Leadership framed the issue as strategic and urgent: the current national portfolio has become less competitive, less compelling to members, and more vulnerable to outside aggregators that are capturing value on top of weak national awards.

The near-term solution is to stand up a **manual deal desk / governance team** that can review national contract decisions now, rather than waiting for future automation. The group is also expected to help define what a future **AI/agentic decision-support capability** should evaluate.

A major recurring theme was that contracts should be designed around **actual member purchasing behavior and activation likelihood**, not legacy tier structures, historical assumptions, or theoretical behavior-shaping models. Leadership emphasized that this is about restoring **member value creation first**, with expected downstream benefits in contract activation, supplier economics, and member growth.
